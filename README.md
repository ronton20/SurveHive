# SurveHive

> **Living document.** This README tracks the current game vision and design as it evolves. Update it whenever scope, story, or systems change — see `CLAUDE.md` for the update policy.

## Elevator Pitch

A worker bee breaks free of his hive queen's corrupted mind-control and must fight through swarms of his former colony to escape — only to discover the queen wasn't the source of the corruption at all. An alien invasion is brainwashing the world's creatures one species at a time, and our hero must grow from a fleeing survivor into the world's last line of defense.

## Story & Setting

- **The Colony**: The player is a worker bee, one of countless drones loosely bound to the hive queen's will — never questioning it, just doing the job to survive.
- **The Corruption**: The queen's mind is corrupted, twisting her from ruler to weapon. Every bee under her control turns violently aggressive, attacking anything not under her command.
- **The Break**: The player is somehow free of her control. To the corrupted hive, that makes him the nearest, most obvious enemy — his entire colony turns on him.
- **The Escape**: The opening arc is a desperate fight through the beehive itself, surviving hordes of former hivemates to reach the outside world.
- **The Reveal**: Outside the hive, it becomes clear the queen wasn't a unique case — an alien invasion is brainwashing living things across the world in an attempt at conquest. What began as a survival story becomes a fight to push back the invasion (and maybe save the world along the way).

## Gameplay Overview

SurveHive is a **Vampire Survivors-style horde survival game**: the player auto-attacks endless waves of enemies while manually focusing on movement and positioning, growing more powerful over the course of a run and, over multiple runs, permanently stronger via meta-progression.

### Worlds

The game is structured into distinct **Worlds**, each a themed run environment with its own enemy roster and narrative beat:

| World | Theme | Example enemies |
|---|---|---|
| Beehive | Escaping the corrupted colony | Worker bees, swarmlings (packs), warrior bees, spitter bees (ranged), bomber bees (suicide AoE), queen's guard, queen's royal guard (miniboss), Queen Bee (boss) |
| Garden | Fleeing into the wider world | Corrupted insects |
| Woods | Deeper into corrupted nature | Corrupted animals |
| City | Civilization has fallen too | TBD |
| Alien Ship | Confronting the source | Aliens |

Each world's enemy roster is organized into **ranks** — common trash mobs scale up to tougher variants, then minibosses, then a world-ending boss (e.g. Beehive: worker bee → warrior bee → spitter bee → queen's guard → queen's royal guard → Queen Bee). Beyond raw stats, ranks vary in **behavior** — chasers, swarming packs, ranged attackers that keep their distance, and suicide bombers that fuse and explode.

### Core Loop (per run)

1. Drop into a world and survive as enemy hordes escalate over time.
2. Auto-attack targets the nearest enemy automatically — no manual aim/shoot.
3. Killing enemies grants **EXP** (leveling) and, from certain enemies, a **run currency**.
4. Leveling up offers a choice of **3 skills/abilities/power-ups**, plus a small automatic bump to base stats.
5. Survive escalating waves, reach world milestones (minibosses, boss), and eventually clear or fall in the world.

### Meta-Progression (roguelite)

Currency (honey) earned during a run banks between runs and is spent in a **flat stat shop** of permanent upgrades — six escalating-cost tracks: Thick Comb Walls (max HP), Royal Jelly Diet (damage), Stronger Wings (move speed), Rapid Reflexes (attack speed), Nectar Scent (pickup range), and Honey Hoarder (honey gain). Purchases persist across app launches via a JSON save and apply to the bee at the start of every run — the roguelite backbone tying individual runs into a larger campaign of growth. (Shop UI arrives with the menus in Phase 4B; the shop is designed for a future hive-tree redesign.)

Alongside honey sits **Royal Jelly**, a premium currency earned in deliberately small fixed amounts — boss kills, one-time per-difficulty first clears, and **achievement rewards** (Phase 5D) — that no gain multiplier ever scales. It banks with the save and buys **hero cosmetics** in the Hive Style panel (Phase 5C) — at list price any time, or discounted through the daily-rotating **Deals** panel (Phase 5E); revives are a possible future sink.

### Controls

- **Mobile**: floating on-screen joystick — it appears where the finger first touches, tracks the drag from that origin, and vanishes on release. No joystick UI is shown otherwise.
- **PC**: WASD / arrow keys. No joystick UI is shown on PC (click-to-move was removed — it added nothing over keyboard movement).
- **Attacks**: fully automatic, always targeting the nearest enemy — the player's manual input is movement/positioning only.

## Art Direction

The target look is **2D top-down pixel art** in the Vampire Survivors mould: side-view sprites that flip left/right on a top-down plane (exactly how the PixelFantasy monsters are authored), high-pace action, and bee/honey-themed skill spectacle.

**Rendering foundation (locked):**
- **PPU 16** everywhere; all imported sprites `Point (no filter)`, `Compression: None`, mipmaps off.
- **URP 2D Pixel Perfect Camera**, reference resolution **320×180** (integer 6× at 1080p), upscale render texture on for crisp VFX rotation.
- **Bloom** (URP post, Global Volume) with a high threshold so only deliberately-bright VFX pixels bloom — "magic honey" pop without smearing the pixel art. *Deferred — set up but not yet dialled in (see TODO).* Optional later: 2D lights for hive-interior mood.

**Honey / hive palette** (UI retints, VFX tints, backgrounds):

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Honey gold | `#FFC30B` | primary accent |
| Amber | `#F5A623` | secondary accent |
| Wax | `#E8D8A0` | light surfaces / text |
| Comb brown | `#8C5A2B` | mid surfaces |
| Deep hive brown | `#3A2416` | backgrounds |
| Danger red | `#D9483B` | damage / warnings |
| Poison green | `#7CB518` | poison / pollen |
| Royal purple | `#7B2D8B` | queen / royal-rank accent |

**Custom-art phase (later) — recommended tools:** [Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org/) (~$20, or free self-compiled; the project already imports `.aseprite` with animation clips) for the hero bee, Queen boss, honey VFX, and hive tileset — free alternatives are Libresprite / Pixelorama; Tilesetter or Aseprite for a honeycomb floor; ChipTone / jsfxr for extra retro SFX and Audacity for trimming. (The current SFX are synthesized in-repo — see `Assets/Audio/CREDITS.md`.)

## Development Status

**Milestone 1 (Beehive vertical slice) is implemented and playable in-editor.** Open `Assets/Scenes/MainMenu.unity` and press Play for the full menu → run loop (or `Beehive.unity` to jump straight into a run).

What exists now:
- Core architecture: input abstraction (WASD, click-to-move, and simulated on-screen joystick behind one interface), zero-GC nearest-enemy auto-targeting, pooled enemies/pickups/projectiles/VFX, EXP/leveling with a 3-choice skill pick-up screen, a run currency wallet, and a `IMetaProgressionStore` seam backed by the persistent save (Phase 4A).
- One world, the **Beehive**, with six data-driven trash/elite enemy ranks (Worker Bee, Swarmling, Warrior Bee, Spitter Bee, Bomber Bee, Queen's Guard — same `EnemyController` script and shared bee rig, different `EnemyStatsSO` data driving tint/scale/stats) spawned by an escalating wave spawner.
- **Enemy variety** (Phase 4) — enemies stop being uniformly chase-and-touch; each archetype is one small behavior component layered on the shared controller:
  - **Spitter Bee** (4A, venom green, unlocks 1:30): **kites to a firing band** — fleeing when the player closes in, chasing when they escape, orbiting in between (`RangedAttack` + the pure `RangedSteering` decision) — then stops, pulses a pink telegraph, and fires a hostile stinger from the shared `EnemyProjectile` pool; shot damage rides the run's contact-damage scaling. Carries a small **magic shield** (the 3B interleave) and is weak in melee — diving it is the counter-play.
  - **Bomber Bee** (4B, hot orange, unlocks 2:30): **rushes fast, fuses, explodes** — inside trigger range it stops and pulses a rapid orange fuse for half a second, then detonates an AoE blast (2.5× its scaled contact damage) with the pack explosion VFX. **Dying also detonates it**, so melee-range kills stay dangerous and ranged kills (or knockback — it's easy to shove) are the counter-play. Stuns hold the fuse rather than defusing it.
  - **Swarmling** (4C, pale blue, unlocks 1:00): a tiny, fast, 8-HP rank that spawns in **packs of 6** (the wave table's new `packSize`) and weaves a per-instance sine wobble (`SwarmMovement`) so the pack fans out into a living cloud that pressures by numbers, not stats.
- **Real pixel art & animation** (Phase 1): player and enemies use the PixelFantasy animated bee rig (idle/run/attack/hit/die clips via a shared Animator + SpriteLibrary/SpriteResolver skinning), rendered through a URP Pixel Perfect Camera at PPU 16 with a 320×180 reference resolution. Sprites flip to face movement/targets.
- **Game feel** (Phase 1): white hit-flash on damage (custom `SurveHive/SpriteFlash` URP shader driven by MaterialPropertyBlock), projectile knockback with per-rank resistance, camera shake on player damage, micro hit-stop on elite kills (deferring to the central `GamePause`), enemy **death animations** (the rig's Death frames play on an inert corpse via `DeathAnimation` driving the SpriteResolver directly, then the pooled instance self-releases) plus a one-shot particle death-poof (the VFX pack's legacy materials were bulk-converted to URP).
- **Hive-themed UI** (Phase 1): DEVNIK pixel UI kit (auto-sliced from its unsplit sheet by scanning pixel regions) tinted in a honey palette, all text on the BoldPixels TMP font (legacy UI.Text fully removed), skill-choice cards with icons, HUD health/EXP bars, currency counter with honey-drop icon, kill counter, and a run timer. Both canvases scale from a **landscape 1920×1080 reference (match height)** so the UI fits a PC screen and reads at full size on 1080p/1440p (Phase 3B-2a — replacing the mobile-era portrait reference that shrank everything). The player **health bar reads danger at a glance** (Phase 3B-2d): the fill is health-graded green → honey amber → danger red, carries a numeric HP readout, pulses below 25% HP, and a lagging "damage trail" flashes the chunk a hit took.
- **Power-up lanes** (Combat 2.0): the level-up offer is split into three lanes, each with a distinct-pick cap and a card banner, and each pick tagged with an element (physical/fire/poison/electric/frost/honey). **Passives** (cap 5) enhance the player — move speed, max HP, **armor** (damage-taken reduction), attack power/speed, ability cooldown, **ability power** (scales actives), crit chance/damage, lifesteal, magnet. **Enhancements** (cap 3) modify the basic attack — Multishot (more projectiles, softer each, ~1.5× total), Piercing Stinger (pierce, penalty that vanishes at max), and elemental stingers (Burning/Poison DoT, Frost freeze, Shock bounce). **Abilities** (cap 5) are the auto-firing active skills. Once a lane is full, only its owned picks keep being offered (`LaneEligibility`).
- **Active skill arsenal** (Phase 2 + Combat 2.0): 8 auto-firing weapons acquirable on level-up alongside the baseline stinger auto-attack, each with a 5-level growth table (damage/cooldown/count/area/status chance) in an `ActiveSkillSO` asset and a distinct delivery: **Stinger Barrage** (radial ring of stingers — now pierces), **Honey Splash** (lobbed glob → slowing honey puddle), **Pollen Cloud** (poisoning aura around the player with a visible radius ring), **Static Wings** (arc chaining between enemies with a stun chance), **Ember Sting** (homing bolt that explodes and burns), **Frost Nova** (radial frost shards that freeze), **Ball Lightning** (radial orbs that stun), and **Honey Bomb** (homing explosion that slows). Damage scales with the Ability Power passive. One `ActiveSkillManager` runs them all from fixed arrays; projectiles/zones/VFX are fully pooled. (The old Piercing Lance active was retired — piercing now lives on the basic attack as the Piercing Stinger enhancement.)
- **Status effects** (Phase 2): burn (DoT, refresh on reapply), poison (DoT, stacking potency with a cap), slow (move-speed multiplier), freeze (hard stop that breaks early on a damage threshold), and stun (full stop + no contact damage, with diminishing returns on elite ranks). Pure-logic `StatusEffectBuffer` (fixed slots, zero allocations) per enemy behind a `StatusEffectReceiver` that ticks DoT with colored damage numbers. **Status readability** (Phase 2A): every status has a signature tint (burn ember-orange, poison toxic-green, slow dusk-blue, freeze ice-cyan, stun spark-yellow, cold frost-blue) applied through the SpriteFlash shader's `_Tint` via MaterialPropertyBlock — renderer.color is keyframed by the rig's animation clips, so direct writes (including the old rank tints) were being silently clobbered; the shader path survives it. Priority when stacked: hard CC > DoTs > movement debuffs, and two-plus statuses **pulse between the top two tints**. Hit flashes are hue-shifted toward the active status so the cue reads even mid-flash in dense combat.
- **Damage typing** (Phase 3A): every damage application carries a **physical/magic** `DamageType` through `DamageService` and `IDamageable.TakeDamage`. The basic attack and its enhancements, enemy contact, and enemy projectiles are physical; elemental abilities and status DoTs are magic, with each ability's type stamped on its `ActiveSkillSO` matching its card element (physical element ⇔ physical damage). Groundwork for enemy defenses (shields/armor reading the incoming type) and elemental set effects.
- **Enemy defenses** (Phase 3B): elites and bosses carry defensive layers beyond HP, applied as an ordered **shield → armor → HP** pipeline (`EnemyDefense`, registered as the typed absorber + mitigator on the enemy's `HealthComponent`). A **physical shield** soaks only physical damage (magic bypasses it), a **magic shield** soaks only magic, and **armor** %-reduces the physical damage that gets past shields — so raw single-type damage stops being the only answer. Per-rank data on `EnemyStatsSO` (shields scale with the run's health curve): Queen's Guard elites have a magic shield + armor, the Royal Guard miniboss a physical shield + armor, the Queen both shields + armor; workers/warriors stay clean. The floating health bar tints steel-blue/violet while a physical/magic shield holds.
- **Elemental set effects** (Phase 3C): committing to one element pays off — owning **2 / 3 / 4+** enhancements+abilities of the same element unlocks escalating set tiers, authored as one `SetBonusSO` per element: **Wildfire** (fire — longer, then harder-ticking burns), **Virulence** (poison), **Overcharge** (electric — up to 2× stun duration), **Deep Chill** (frost — sturdier freezes, stronger chills), **Sticky Sweet** (honey — stickier, longer slows), and **Sharp Stingers** (physical — +6/12/20% basic-attack damage). The run-scoped `ElementSets` service recounts pieces after every pick and amplifies statuses at the single application choke point. Set state surfaces where picks are decided, not on the combat HUD: new offer cards show their element's set progress and what the next tier grants, the offer panel lists active tiers with their effects, and the pause menu's build panel has a SET BONUSES section (pieces, active effect, next threshold).
- **Set signature effects** (Phase 2B): the **4-piece (top) tier** adds a build-defining signature on top of the potency/duration scaling — one per element, most triggered by an enemy's death while it carries that element's status. **Wildfire** spreads Burn to the nearest enemy; **Deep Chill** shatters chilled/frozen enemies for a burst of AoE magic damage (25% of the victim's max HP); **Overcharge** arcs the Stun to a nearby enemy; **Virulence** leaves a lingering toxic pool; **Sticky Sweet** leaves a sticky slow zone; and **Sharp Stingers** makes the basic attack **execute** enemies below 15% HP outright. The `ElementalSetSignatures` dispatcher fires off `EnemyController`'s death; pools/slicks reuse the honey-puddle zone pool and the execute is a cached one-field check on the attack path (zero-GC). The unlocked signature shows as a `✦` line under its set on the offer panel.
- **Passives**: Swift Wings (move speed), Thicker Chitin (max HP), Waxen Plating (armor), Potent Venom (damage %), Keen Eye (crit chance), Deadly Precision (crit damage), Nectar Drain (lifesteal), Nectar Sense (pickup magnet radius), Hyper Metabolism (active-skill cooldown reduction), Royal Focus (ability power). Crits roll centrally in `DamageService` (gold oversized damage numbers) — base crit chance is **0%**, so crit is a deliberate build investment: Keen Eye grants 5/10/15/20/30% cumulative over its 5 levels, and a permanent meta-shop crit track (up to +40%) arrives with the shop expansion. Lifesteal heals off every damage source; armor mitigates incoming damage. (Twin Stingers / Longer Stinger moved to the Enhancement lane.)
- **Rarity & lucky picks** (Phase 2): skills carry a Common/Rare/Epic tier that drives weighted offer selection (weights 1 / 0.65 / 0.22, sampled without replacement) and the card frame color; each offered card has a small chance to roll **lucky** — a distinct green card that grants +2 levels in one pick.
- Skills have per-run **levels**: the choice card shows the growth (e.g. `Lv. 1 → Lv. 2`), and skills that hit their level cap stop being offered. Some stats are capped (e.g. projectile count maxes at 5). Each level-up also grants a small automatic bump to base stats (max health, damage, attack speed — move speed grows only via the Swift Wings power-up).
- **Run structure** (Phase 3): each run follows a 10-minute `StageConfigSO` timeline driven by a `StageDirector` — base spawn rate escalates on a curve (1× → 3.5×) on top of stat scaling, a **surround-ring strong wave** hits at 25%, the **Queen's Royal Guard** miniboss at 50% (telegraphed charge attack — she stops, pulses red, then dashes), a **directional flood wave** at 75%, and the **Queen Bee** at 100%. A HUD progress bar under the run timer shows siren/skull/crown markers for what's coming. While a miniboss or the Queen is alive the timeline and regular enemy spawns **freeze** (the boss fight becomes the focus — her own summons still spawn), resuming when the miniboss is killed.
- **Boss & wave drama** (Combat 2.0 Phase 2): every strong wave and boss/miniboss telegraphs with a **5-second "incoming" warning** banner + countdown. A **miniboss kill is a reward beat** — a guaranteed lucky (+2) level-up offer plus a burst of EXP. And any **boss/miniboss death plays a cinematic**: 0.25× slow-motion for the death animation with the player invulnerable, a shockwave + screen shake, holding the timeline resume / victory / reward until the beat finishes.
- **Queen Bee boss fight** (Phase 3): royal-tinted BossPack1 dragon body (until custom art) rotating three telegraphed patterns — summoning corrupted workers, a radial stinger burst (enemy projectiles), and a charging sweep. Bosses announce with a name banner + screen shake and get a HUD boss health bar. **Killing the Queen clears the world** — the first winnable run. She also **enrages** (Phase 1A round 2): past a minute of fight time her damage ramps to 2.5× and her patterns accelerate, so the fight can't be won by kiting-and-chipping forever — you beat her with a build, not with patience (the regular spawn drip freezes during boss fights, which otherwise made an endless retreat-and-heal war viable).
- **Item drops** (Phase 3): elites and bosses drop pooled world items — **Honey Jar** (heal 30% max HP), **Magnet** (vacuums every pickup for 3s), **Wax Shield** (absorbs the next 3 hits, shown as a ring around the bee), **Royal Bomb** (screen nuke with a big purple blast + hit-stop). Drop chances live on `EnemyStatsSO` (trash 1–3%, elites 12%, bosses always).
- **Run results** (Phase 3): both the death and victory screens show the run summary — time survived, kills, level reached, and honey banked — and the run's currency banks into the meta store on both paths. Restart via the RETRY button or R; the HIVE button returns to the menu.
- **Save & meta shop core** (Phase 4A): a versioned JSON save at `Application.persistentDataPath` (safe-written via temp-file swap; corrupt/missing saves fall back to a fresh start) persists banked honey, purchased upgrade ranks, settings, and best-run stats (best time/kills/level, runs played, victories). Six `MetaUpgradeSO` shop upgrades with escalating costs apply permanently at run start via `MetaUpgradeApplier` (flat +max HP and flat +damage so early ranks are felt; % move speed, attack speed, pickup radius, honey gain). Purchases go through the pure `MetaShop`/`MetaProgressionState` logic against the `IMetaProgressionStore` seam.
- **Meta shop expansion** (Phase 1C of the current plan): **13 upgrades** — the six above plus EXP gain, ability damage, active-skill cooldown cut, **crit chance** (+2%/rank, 20 ranks, 40% cap on the 0% base — stacking with Keen Eye's 30% for a 70% ceiling), crit damage, item drop rate, and **power-up rerolls ("Waggle Dance")**. Reroll rank = per-run stock (max 3, refilled each run): on the level-up screen each card shows a REROLL button plus a remaining count (hidden until a rank is owned); a reroll replaces just that card with a fresh eligible pick that never duplicates one on screen, and won't spend a charge if there's nothing else to offer. Deliberately expensive: 400 / 1,520 / 5,776 honey.
- **Meta shop tab layout** (Phase 3B-1 of the current plan): the Hive Upgrades screen is organized into three **category tabs** on the left — **Combat** (Damage, Attack Speed, Crit Chance, Crit Damage, Ability DMG, Cooldown Cut), **Survival** (Max HP, Move Speed, Pickup Range), and **Utility** (Honey Gain, EXP Gain, Item Drop Rate, Rerolls). Picking a tab shows a grid of just that category's upgrade icons (each with its `rank/max`) and auto-selects the first; a detail pane up top shows the selected upgrade — icon, name, description, rank, the exact value change (e.g. `+25 → +50 Max HP`), and the BUY button, whose label **is the price** (honey icon + number; "MAX" when topped out; greys out when unaffordable). Categories are derived from each upgrade's stat, so the tabs stay correct as the roster grows. Currencies render as their **icons** wherever the UI mentions them — a TMP sprite asset (honey drop + royal-jelly comb cell) registered as the default, so any text can inline `<sprite name="honey"/"jelly">`; the shop header balances and the results screen's banked/earned lines use it.
- **Codex** (Phase 5A + the 2026-07-11 depth pass): a main-menu **CODEX** encyclopedia of everything the player has encountered, in the shop's tabbed layout — **Power-Ups** (all 26 cards, sectioned **Passives / Enhancements / Abilities**), **Sets** (the 6 elemental set bonuses with their tier tables), **Enemies** (sectioned **per world**; each entry is a hand-written behavior blurb — no HP/DMG numbers, those scale with difficulty and run time), and **Items** (the 4 world drops). The entry grid scrolls, so tabs grow cleanly as worlds are added. Undiscovered entries show as black silhouettes with a "???" detail until met; once discovered, a power-up's detail lists **what every level grants** (per-level stat increments, or the active skill's full DMG/count/area/cooldown/status growth table). A run-scoped `CodexTracker` records the first pick of each power-up, the first activation of each set tier, the first spawn of each enemy rank (one hook in `EnemySpawner.SpawnAt` covers the drip, waves, bosses, and summons), and the first pickup of each item, then flushes the batch to the save in one write at scene teardown (no mid-combat file IO — a crash loses only that run's discoveries). Unlocks persist in save **v5** (`codexIds`); a DISCOVERED `n/total` counter tracks completion.
- **Royal Jelly** (Phase 5B): the premium currency — earned only in small fixed amounts (**+1 per miniboss kill, +3 per Queen kill**, and a **one-time first-clear bonus per stage+difficulty**: 10/15/20/25 for Easy→Extreme), never scaled by the honey gain multipliers, banked on both death and victory, and persisted in save **v6** (`bankedJelly`). The shop header shows the jelly balance beside honey, and the results screen adds a `Royal Jelly +N` line on runs that earned some. Its first sink is the Hive Style cosmetics panel (5C, below); achievements (5D) pay small jelly rewards, and the daily Deals panel (5E) discounts cosmetics.
- **Hive Style — character customization** (Phase 5C + same-day playtest follow-up): a main-menu **STYLE** panel (the shop's tabbed mold) where Royal Jelly buys purely-visual hero cosmetics across three slots — **Colors** (5 basic body tints, 3–5 jelly; placeholders — real per-region "transformable" sprite variants are planned once final hero art exists), **Hats** (Honey Crown / Dapper Top Hat / Daisy Clip overlays, 8–15 jelly), and **Stingers**, which re-skin the **auto-attack projectile**: **3 shapes × 3 colors** (Needle / Barb / Blade × Amber / Sapphire / Venom — one neutral sprite per shape, the color is a runtime tint), grouped in the tab under one section header per shape, priced by shape base (6/10/14) + color premium (0/+2/+4). Each tab leads with a free **DEFAULT** cell; buying auto-equips; the equipped entry wears a gold badge; and the live hero preview is **try-before-you-buy** — it always wears the currently *selected* entry in its slot (bought or not), with the other slots showing what's equipped. Cosmetics are data (`CosmeticSO` roster + catalog, authored by `CosmeticsBuilder`; art is placeholder pixel art tracked in `ASSET_GENERATION.md` §2.10). In-run, a `CosmeticApplier` on the hero applies the equipped set once at spawn: the color tints the body through the SpriteFlash shader's `_Tint` (animation-clip-proof, merges with hit-flash), the hat is an overlay child renderer on the Body rig so it flips with facing, and the stinger publishes to a static `StingerSkin` that every pooled auto-attack projectile applies on spawn (zero-GC; enemy/skill projectiles untouched). Owned + equipped ids persist in save **v7**; the default look is an empty slot, so old saves migrate untouched.
- **Achievements** (Phase 5D): 11 local-first achievements whose conditions all ride signals the game already emits — kills in a single run (1/250/1,000), hero level (10/20), surviving 5 minutes, activating any elemental set bonus or maxing one, and clearing a stage on any/Hard/Extreme difficulty. Each pays a small Royal Jelly reward (1–15, keeping the 5B scarcity), and the Extreme clear also grants the Honey Crown hat outright. A run-scoped `AchievementTracker` checks the still-locked slice of the catalog against live stats (zero-GC in combat); an unlock pops an in-run **toast banner** (name + reward, queued if several land at once) immediately, while rewards and save writes flush at run end / scene teardown like the codex. A main-menu **AWARDS** panel lists every achievement with its reward and an `UNLOCKED n/total` counter. Unlocks persist in save **v8** (`unlockedAchievementIds`). **Steam** is a later shim: the tracker reports through a `IAchievementBackend` seam whose default `LocalAchievementBackend` is a no-op — a Steamworks backend slots in at boot without touching game code.
- **Daily Deals — rotating cosmetics shop** (Phase 5E): a main-menu **DEALS** panel that features up to **3 not-yet-owned cosmetics per local day at 30% off** (rounded half-up, never below 1 jelly) — the whole 5C catalog stays buyable at list price in Hive Style, so the rotation is a reason to check in, not a gate. The pick is **deterministic and serverless**: seeded by the local date (yyyymmdd) over the unowned slice of the catalog, then **frozen into the save** (v9: `dailyDealDay` + `dailyDealIds`) on first sight, so the day's selection survives restarts and buying one deal never re-rolls the others (bought deals show **SOLD** until rollover). Each card shows the cosmetic's icon, name, flavor text, and the struck-through list price next to the deal price; buying spends jelly and auto-equips exactly like Hive Style. A once-per-second countdown ("NEW DEALS IN HH:MM:SS") tracks the local-midnight rollover and re-rolls live if midnight passes with the panel open. Once every cosmetic is owned the panel says so instead of showing empty cards. Pure pick/price/rollover logic lives in `Progression/RotatingShop` (EditMode-tested); the panel is built by the additive `RotatingShopBuilder`.
- **Main menu & scene flow** (Phase 4B): a generated `MainMenu` scene (the boot scene in build settings) in the pixel kit — home (Play / Hive Upgrades / Codex / Style / Deals / Awards / Settings / Quit), world select (Beehive playable; Garden and Woods shown locked; a live difficulty picker — see below), and the **Hive Upgrades shop** (reworked into a tabbed layout in Phase 3B-1 — see above). Death and victory screens gained **RETRY** and **HIVE** buttons (reload run / back to menu); tap-anywhere restart was removed in favor of the buttons (R still restarts from the keyboard).
- **Stage difficulty** (Phase 1B of the current plan): the world-select screen has a working four-tier difficulty dropdown — **Easy / Normal / Hard / Extreme**, each with an icon (placeholders for now). Tiers are data-driven via a single `DifficultySO` tier table (`Assets/Data/Progression/DifficultySettings.asset`): each row scales enemy HP and contact/projectile damage (Easy 0.75×/0.75× → Extreme 2.25×/1.9×), optionally the spawn rate (Hard 1.15×, Extreme 1.3×), and compensates with a **honey-gain multiplier** (Easy 0.75× → Extreme 2.25×) applied to every pickup on top of the meta-shop gain upgrade. The multipliers apply once at run start on top of the per-minute ramp, covering the regular spawn drip, strong waves, the miniboss, and the Queen alike. The last-selected tier persists in the save and restores on the next boot. **Tiers are earned**: Easy/Normal are always open, Hard unlocks by clearing The Beehive on Normal, and Extreme by clearing it on Hard plus clearing the next stage (the Garden) on Normal — locked tiers show "— LOCKED" in the dropdown, refuse selection, and surface their unlock tasks in a hover tooltip (completed tasks checked + struck through) via the game's **shared tooltip system** — a single `TooltipUI` per scene on its own top-sorted overlay canvas that shows on hover, sizes itself to its text, follows the mouse, and clamps to the screen (a generic `TooltipTrigger` component puts it on any UI element; planned to carry status-effect/set-effect info later). Victories record per-stage/per-difficulty clear flags in the save (v3).
- **Pause & settings** (Phase 4C): ESC or the HUD pause button freezes the run completely (every spawner/cooldown runs on scaled time) and opens resume / settings / abandon — abandoning banks the run's honey and returns to the menu. A shared settings block (music + SFX sliders, vibration and quality cycle-buttons) lives in both the main menu and the pause menu; changes apply live through the audio service and persist to the save immediately. The pause menu never opens over the level-up, death, or victory freezes. **Enhanced options** (Phase 3C): both settings panels also carry five feedback-layer toggles — **enemy HP bars, damage numbers, screen shake, hit-stop, and status colors** — laid out as a second column beside the audio/general controls. Each toggle gates its system at a single entry point via the static `FeedbackSettings` live copy (pushed by the save store on load and on every settings save), so flipping one mid-run takes effect instantly — even on already-spawned pooled enemies — and survives a restart (save schema v4; older saves migrate with every layer on).
- **Audio** (Phase 5A): a scene-scoped `AudioService` (behind an `IAudioService` seam, per the project's DIP conventions) — a small round-robin pool of SFX `AudioSource`s (so overlapping one-shots layer instead of cutting each other off, each with its own pitch jitter) plus one looping music source, both driven by the settings sliders. SFX are **original, procedurally synthesized** to fit the bee/honey pixel-art theme (buzzy amplitude-modulated sawtooths for combat, chiptune arpeggios for level-up/victory, honey "bloops" for pickups, distinct gestures per skill) — generated by `Tools/Audio/synth.py`, covering hit, kill, currency pickup, level-up, player hurt/death, victory, UI clicks, a subtler UI hover tick, boss stinger bursts, and each of the 6 active skills firing. Two looping CC0 music tracks (menu + run) round it out; all sources are documented in `Assets/Audio/CREDITS.md`. Hit/kill carry a per-sound min-interval throttle so an AoE hitting a whole horde reads as a texture rather than a machine-gun wall. Aura ticks (Pollen Cloud) and EXP-orb pickups are deliberately excluded from SFX — both fire many times a second and would flood the mix. Every clip maps 1:1 to an `SfxId`, so swapping in AI-generated (e.g. ElevenLabs) or sourced sounds for final polish is just drop-a-file + re-run the Phase 5A pass.
- Player stats include an **attack speed** multiplier (higher = faster firing) that scales on level-up and is open to future modifiers.
- Enemies scale up over the course of a run — health and contact damage grow per minute (tunable in the wave config), so upgrades stay meaningful.
- EXP/currency pickups drift toward the player within a pickup radius (magnet-style, standard for the genre) instead of requiring a pixel-perfect walk-over. All pickups — motes and item drops alike — share one attraction system (`PickupMotion`): the same base radius, the Nectar Sense multiplier, and the Magnet drop's vacuum.
- **EXP orbs** scale with the kill: reward = per-rank base (dominant) + a share of the enemy's scaled max HP, so tougher and later kills feed more. Orbs dropped near an existing orb **merge into it**, and orbs restyle by stored value — bigger and hotter-tinted (green → cyan → orange → royal purple) on a neutral white sprite.
- **Readable upgrades**: level-up cards for skills you already own show the exact numbers changing (e.g. `Basic Attack DMG 10 → 11`, `Projectiles 6 → 7`, `Cooldown 2.6s → 2.4s`) instead of flavor text; new skills keep their descriptions. Difficulty scaling steps per whole minute so early enemies die in the intended hit counts (a 20 HP worker takes exactly two 10-damage stings in minute 0).
- Floating health bars above enemies (Phase 3B-2d: bigger and framed for contrast, shield-tinted steel-blue/violet while a shield holds) and floating damage-number popups on hit. The **boss** health bar carries the same lagging damage trail and shifts from royal purple toward red as the Queen nears death.
- Death & restart: when the player's health hits zero the run ends — the game freezes, a "YOU DIED" screen appears, and pressing R / tapping reloads the run. Level-up pacing and per-level bonuses are tunable via the `LevelCurve` asset and the level-up controller.
- Projectile/pickup sprites are still small code-generated pixel art (stinger dart, nectar mote, honey drop) pending custom Aseprite art.
- Not yet built: other worlds (Garden/Woods/City/Alien Ship), the rest of the balance/curve tuning pass, mobile sanity pass, and a localization seam.
